================================================================
  DND NOTICEBOARD GENERATOR — MANUAL ROLLING GUIDE
================================================================
  How to use these tables with real dice at the table.

  DICE NEEDED
  -----------
  d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, d20, d100 (or two d10s as percentile)

================================================================
  STEP 1 — ROLL QUALITY
================================================================
  Roll 1d10 to find the noticeboard's quality and reputation.

  1–2   Underground   Criminal or black-market board
  3–4   Decrepit      Neglected, unreliable
  5–6   Standard      Average town board
  7–8   Good          Well-maintained, reputable
  9–10  Pristine      Official, well-funded

================================================================
  STEP 2 — ROLL SIZE
================================================================
  Roll 1d10 to find how many notices are posted.

  1–2   Tiny      Roll 1d4   notices
  3–4   Small     Roll 2d4   notices
  5–6   Average   Roll 3d4–1 notices (minimum 2)
  7–8   Large     Roll 4d4   notices
  9–10  Enormous  Roll 5d4–1 notices (minimum 4)

================================================================
  STEP 3 — CONTRACT TYPE PER NOTICE (roll d100)
================================================================
  For each notice, roll d100 on the table for your quality.

================================================================
  STEP 5 — ILLEGITIMATE CONTRACTS
================================================================

  5a. ROLL SEED (d100)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  01–75   Illegitimate False Premise   (uses contract table + real purpose)
  76–00   Illegitimate Betrayal        (uses contract table + betrayal)

  For the contract table, first roll d3 (or d6, halved) to pick:
    1  Dangerous Contract  →  roll on the Dangerous Contract table (4e)
    2  Social Contract     →  roll on the Social Contract table (4g)
    3  Investigation Contract → roll on the Investigation table (4h)


  5b. REAL PURPOSE TABLE (d12)
  Used for the "False Premise" seed.
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  1–3    completing the contract implicates the party in a larger problem
  4–6    completing the job distracts from the real crime
  7–8    the job is to test the party for future work
  9–10   a rival party is already involved
  11     performing the job leads to evidence being stolen or destroyed
  12     performing the job starts a feud between two groups


  5c. BETRAYAL TABLE (d12)
  Used for the "Betrayal" seed.
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  1–2    the employer will refuse to pay
  3–4    the employer vanishes during the job
  5–6    the city watch / enemies have already been tipped off
  7–8    completing the contract forces party into a larger problem
  9      the party is locked in, trapped, or abandoned during the job
  10     the job is an ambush
  11     the payment is {paymentCondition}*
  12     the party is paid, but accepting payment implicates them

  * Roll d6 for Payment Condition:
    1–3 fake   4–5 stolen   6 cursed

================================================================
  REWARD CALCULATION (optional)
================================================================
  Each contract has a base GP reward set by the contract entry.
  Employer and complication entries carry a reward modifier (%).

  Final GP = base reward × (1 + sum of all modifiers / 100)
             + random drift of ±15%

  Round down. Minimum 1 GP.

  If a weird payment is added, append it to the GP reward:
  e.g. "150 GP + a favour from a person of power"
